# aah.monster — Positioning Update: Responsibility Angle
*v1 — May 2026*
*For: About page section, service pages, or standalone copy block*

---

## Core line

**your name is on the brand. so is mine.**

---

## Copy block (About page / service intro)

---

you know why people pay lawyers?

not for the documents. the documents are a commodity. any first-year associate can produce documents.

people pay lawyers because when the lawyer signs off on something, their license is at stake. their career. their name on the bar association wall. that's not performance — that's skin in the game.

that's what you're buying when you work with me.

i'm not a vendor who builds you something and walks away. my name goes on whatever i tell you to do. if the positioning is wrong, i'm the one who told you to run it. if the messaging fails, i wrote the brief. i don't get to say "well, the market was tough."

your name is on the brand. so is mine.

that's what no calls, no retainers, no upsell means in practice: i have to be right the first time. there's no "let's circle back next quarter" to paper over a bad recommendation. the work lands, or it doesn't. and if it doesn't, that's my problem as much as yours.

most brand consultants give you a deck and wish you luck. i give you a diagnosis i'd stake my reputation on.

---

## Shorter version (for hero section / tagline context)

---

most advisors give you a recommendation and bill for the hour.

i give you one i'd put my name on.

your name is on the brand. so is mine.

---

## Usage notes

- The lawyer analogy works as a verbal explanation but shouldn't be stated directly on the site — let the logic land without naming the frame
- "your name is on the brand. so is mine." = most compressed version — works as standalone line under the tagline
- The long block works on the About page between the "no calls" explanation and the service links
- Lowercase throughout — matches existing brand voice
- No em-dashes used
- Passes: no "authentic", no "leverage", no "unique value proposition", no "thought leadership"
