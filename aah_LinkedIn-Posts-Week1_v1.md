## POST 1
*Best for: Monday morning*
*Topic: LinkedIn profile ≠ personal brand*

---

your LinkedIn profile is not your personal brand.

your LinkedIn profile is a form you filled out.

a personal brand is what people say about you when you leave the room.

most C-suite executives have the first.

almost none have the second.

they have a photo, a title, and 800 words about "leading cross-functional teams" that could describe any of the 4 million other people with the same title on LinkedIn.

that's not a brand.

that's a tax form with a headshot.

the irony: these are the same people who spend six figures building their company's brand, because they understand what brand actually does.

they just never applied the same logic to themselves.

the company gets positioning, differentiation, a clear reason to choose it.

the person who runs the company gets a job description and a list of endorsements for "strategic leadership."

you built the brand for the business.

you are the business.

## POST 2
*Best for: Wednesday*
*Topic: what a brand audit looks like in 60 seconds*

---

i can tell in 60 seconds if someone has a personal brand or not.

here's the test.

first line of their bio: job description or positioning statement?

most: "marketing professional with 15+ years of experience driving results across FMCG, finance and tech."

that's a CV opener. it describes your past. a brand describes your future.

second: the last 10 posts.

does this person have a point of view? or are they a relay station for other people's content?

reposting industry news is not content. it's digital filler.

third: the "featured" section.

proof of thinking, or press releases?

most people fail all three.

not because they're bad at their jobs.

because nobody ever told them their LinkedIn profile is a pitch document.

the CMO who nails the first line, the founder who has an actual opinion on record, the VP who publishes thinking not just achievements.

those are the ones getting called first.

## POST 3
*Best for: Friday*
*Topic: why branding consultants love discovery calls (and why I don't do them)*

---

every branding consultant will ask for a discovery call.

some will ask for three.

they'll call it "deep dive" or "alignment session" or "getting to know your vision."

it's none of those things.

it's an audition.

they want to feel you out before they commit to having an opinion on your brand.

i do it differently.

you fill out a form. 15 minutes. you tell me what's working and what isn't.

i read it. i come back to you with a written assessment.

no call. no "let's explore synergies." no 45-minute session where two people decide whether they have chemistry.

C-suite executives and founders hired me this way, paid me €199 to €2,500, and got the work delivered as a document.

turns out async is faster than chemistry.

if you need to feel me out first: aah.monster/free-checklist — 10-minute free brand audit checklist.

if you already know what you need: aah.monster
