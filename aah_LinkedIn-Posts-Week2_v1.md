## POST 4
*Best for: Monday morning*
*Topic: You don't have a personal brand problem. You have an opinion problem.*

---

you don't have a personal brand problem.

you have an opinion problem.

the executives i audit almost always make the same error.

they post. regularly. relevant content. properly formatted.

and zero point of view.

industry news, shared thoughtfully. market updates, followed by "interesting to see how this develops." conference takeaways, neutralized into bullet points.

none of it says anything.

a relay station for other people's thinking is not a personal brand.

it's a PR department with a headshot.

the actual brand is the opinion.

the take that's slightly uncomfortable to say out loud. the prediction you'd put your name on. the thing your comms team would soften before publishing.

most executives have spent 20 years learning to speak without committing to anything.

inside a corporate hierarchy, that's called political intelligence.

on LinkedIn, it's called invisibility.

## POST 5
*Best for: Wednesday*
*Topic: Consistency is not posting 3x a week — it's saying the same thing in 100 different formats.*

---

3 posts a week is not a brand.

it's a schedule.

the platform coaches say: be consistent. show up. stay visible.

they're not wrong. they're just describing the least important part.

consistency of frequency is table stakes.

consistency of message is the brand.

ogilvy wrote the same thing for 40 years. different campaigns, different clients, same obsession with what actually sells.

the executives and founders who became known for something did not do it by being prolific.

they did it by being relentless about one specific idea until their name became synonymous with it.

most LinkedIn profiles are the opposite.

monday motivation. hot take on the latest news. personal story. product update. interview repost. friday reflection.

a slot machine, not a brand.

pick a lane. a narrow, uncomfortably specific lane.

drive it for two years.

then expand.

## POST 6
*Best for: Friday*
*Topic: The invisible executive — the brand that stops at the company boundary.*

---

there is a specific type of person i find fascinating.

runs a €50M company. respected inside their industry. people who've worked with them will vouch immediately.

open their LinkedIn: 200 connections. last post: two years ago.

the brand stops at the company boundary.

outside the org chart: invisible.

this is fine. until it isn't.

board changes. company gets acquired. industry shifts. the next chapter requires walking into rooms where nobody knows their name.

that's when the absence of a personal brand stops being a lifestyle choice and becomes a liability.

you can't build a reputation in a crisis.

you can only spend one you already have.

the executive who built their name before the transition had options.

the one who let the company's brand carry them had none.

your name is an asset.

if nobody outside your org knows what you stand for, the asset is dormant.

dormant assets don't compound.
